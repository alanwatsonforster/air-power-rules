\begin{center}
\textbf{\Large Cover Photograph}
\end{center}

The photograph on the cover shows an A-7A Corsair II of VA-174, “The Argonauts,” preparing to launch from USS \textit{Ranger} during the Vietnam War. 

The photograph is undated but is probably from late 1967 or 1968, during the first combat deployment of the A-7A (see chapter 2 of Mersky \& Berzer, “\href{https://www.ospreypublishing.com/us/us-navy-a7-corsair-ii-units-of-the-vietnam-war-9781782005377/}{US Navy A-7 Corsair II Units of the Vietnam War}”).

The aircraft carries the name of Cdr.\ W.\ Scott Gray, the squadron’s executive officer, and is armed for an Iron Hand mission with AGM-45 Shrike anti-radiation missiles, CBU-75 Sadeye cluster bombs, and AIM-9D Sidewinder air-to-air missiles. 

During the war, twenty-four A-7 pilots were killed in action, three died in operational accidents, four died in captivity, and seven survived captivity and were released in 1973.

The \href{https://www.history.navy.mil/our-collections/photography/numerical-list-of-images/nhhc-series/naval-subjects-collection/l01--aviation/l01-18-04-01-a-7-corsair-ii.html}{photograph} was taken by an unknown USN photographer and is in the public domain.

A line drawing based on this photograph appeared on the cover of the first-edition rules distributed with \emph{The Speed of Heat}.
